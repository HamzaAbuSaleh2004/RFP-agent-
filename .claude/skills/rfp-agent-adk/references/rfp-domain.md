# RFP Domain Knowledge

## RFP Document Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Cover Page | Company name, RFP title, number, issue/due dates |
| Introduction | Why this RFP exists, organizational context |
| Scope of Work | What's being procured — deliverables, standards, timeline |
| Submission Requirements | Format rules, required sections, deadline |
| Evaluation Criteria | How proposals will be scored (weighted rubric) |
| Terms & Conditions | IP, liability, payment, termination, compliance |
| Timeline | Key dates from issue through award |
| Appendices | Requirements matrix, pricing template, forms |

## Evaluation Methods

**Best Value**: Scores technical + cost separately, weighs both. Higher-priced but technically superior can win.

**LPTA (Lowest Price Technically Acceptable)**: Pass/fail on technical, then lowest price wins.

**Qualifications-Based**: Ranked on qualifications first, price negotiated with top-ranked.

## Standard Evaluation Weights

| Criterion | Typical Weight | Adjust When |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Technical Approach | 35% | Technology projects → 40% |
| Past Performance | 20% | New market → reduce to 10% |
| Key Personnel | 15% | Consulting → 25% |
| Cost/Price | 20% | Commodity → 40% |
| Innovation | 10% | R&D projects → 15% |

## Requirements Language

- **"shall"** = mandatory (failure to meet = disqualification)
- **"should"** = desirable (scored but not pass/fail)
- **"may"** = optional (bonus points if offered)

## Bid Evaluation Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Exceeds requirements, innovative, proven approach |
| 4 | Good | Meets all requirements with notable strengths |
| 3 | Acceptable | Meets requirements adequately |
| 2 | Marginal | Partially meets requirements, concerns exist |
| 1 | Poor | Does not meet requirements |

## Country-Specific Considerations

When creating RFPs for international procurement, research agents should verify:
- Local vendor availability and market maturity
- Import/export restrictions and tariffs
- Data sovereignty and residency requirements
- Professional licensing and certification requirements
- Anti-corruption compliance (FCPA, UK Bribery Act)
- Currency and payment infrastructure
- Labor law implications for onsite work
- Environmental regulations
- Language and translation requirements
