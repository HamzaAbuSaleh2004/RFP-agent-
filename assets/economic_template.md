# Economic Template — Standard RFP Financial & Commercial Framework

> Replace the placeholder values (marked with [BRACKETS]) with your company's actual policies.
> These terms are mandatory in all RFPs unless explicitly overridden by a senior procurement officer.

---

## 1. Budget Policy

1.1 All RFPs must include a stated budget ceiling. Bids exceeding the ceiling by more than
**[X]%** will be automatically disqualified unless a written waiver is granted.

1.2 Budget ranges must be validated against current market benchmarks before the RFP is issued.
Finance team sign-off is required for any RFP with a total value exceeding **[100000]**.

1.3 Multi-year contracts must include a cost escalation cap of **[10]% per year**, indexed to
[CPI / agreed index]. Vendors must disclose their escalation assumptions in their bids.

## 2. Pricing & Fee Structure

2.1 Vendors must submit pricing in the following mandatory format:

| Line Item              | Unit | Quantity | Unit Price | Total |
|------------------------|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| [Deliverable / Service]|      |          |           |       |
| Travel & Expenses      | Lump |    1     |           |       |
| Contingency (max 5%)   | %    |          |           |       |
| **Total Contract Value**|     |          |           |       |

2.2 All prices must be stated in **[JOD]**, exclusive of applicable taxes.
Applicable taxes (VAT / GST at current rate) will be paid separately by [COMPANY NAME].

2.3 Fixed-price bids are preferred. If time-and-materials pricing is proposed, vendors must
provide day rates per role and a capped estimate with a not-to-exceed total.

2.4 Prices must remain valid for **[60] days** from the bid submission deadline.

## 3. Payment Schedule

Standard payment milestones — adjust per project as needed:

| Milestone                        | % of Contract Value | Trigger                              |
|----------------------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Contract Signing / Mobilisation  | [10]%               | Signed contract received                |
| Mid-Project Delivery             | [40]%               | Acceptance of agreed interim deliverable|
| Final Delivery                   | [40]%               | Written acceptance of all deliverables  |
| Retention Release                | [10]%               | Defects liability period ends           |

3.1 Payment terms are **Net [30] days** from receipt of a valid, undisputed invoice.

3.2 Invoices must reference the purchase order number and itemise deliverables per the agreed
fee schedule. Incomplete invoices will be returned and the payment clock restarted.

3.3 [COMPANY NAME] reserves the right to withhold payment for deliverables that have not
received formal written acceptance.

---

## 4. Penalty & Incentive Clauses

4.1 **Late Delivery Penalty:** [5]% of the milestone value per calendar week of delay, up to
a maximum of **[10]%** of the total contract value.

4.2 **Quality Failure Penalty:** If a deliverable fails acceptance testing and requires more
than **[2]** revision cycles, a penalty of **[1000]** per additional cycle applies.

4.3 **Early Completion Bonus (optional):** Vendors completing the project **[2] weeks** ahead
of schedule with full acceptance may be eligible for a bonus of **[10]%** of contract value,
at [COMPANY NAME]'s sole discretion.

---

## 5. Cost-Control Requirements

5.1 Vendors must submit a detailed cost breakdown with their bid, showing assumptions for
effort (person-days), rates, and any third-party costs.

5.2 Any change in scope must be agreed in writing via a formal Change Order before work
begins. Costs incurred without an approved Change Order will not be reimbursed.

5.3 Vendors must flag any anticipated cost overrun as soon as it is identified — no later
than when **[10]%** of the budget is consumed. Surprises at invoice time are not acceptable.

5.4 Travel and expense claims must comply with [COMPANY NAME]'s T&E policy:
- Economy class for flights under [3] hours; business class requires prior approval.
- Hotel accommodation capped at **[100] per night**.
- All expenses require original receipts and must be claimed within **30 days** of incurrence.

---

## 6. Financial Qualification Thresholds

To be considered financially qualified, vendors must demonstrate:

| Criterion                         | Minimum Requirement              |
|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Annual Revenue (latest year)      | At least [X]× total contract value |
| Net Income / Operating Profit     | Positive in the last fiscal year |
| Cash & Equivalents                | Sufficient for ≥ 18 months runway|
| Credit Rating (if applicable)     | [MINIMUM RATING, e.g. BBB / A-]  |

Vendors that do not meet these thresholds will be flagged for finance review and may be asked
to provide a parent company guarantee or performance bond.

---

## 7. Currency & Exchange Rate Risk

7.1 All contracts will be denominated in **[BASE CURRENCY]**.

7.2 If a vendor proposes costs in a foreign currency, they must state the exchange rate used
and confirm the rate will be locked at contract signing.

7.3 Exchange rate fluctuation risk beyond **[X]%** over the contract period may be addressed
via a forex adjustment clause, subject to finance team approval.
