# Legal Template — Standard RFP Contractual Framework

> Replace the placeholder values (marked with [BRACKETS]) with your company's actual details
> before issuing any RFP. All clauses below are mandatory unless marked "(optional)".

---

## 1. Governing Law & Jurisdiction

All contracts arising from this RFP shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of [COUNTRY / JURISDICTION]. Any disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction
of the courts of [CITY, COUNTRY].

---

## 2. Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure

2.1 All information shared during the RFP process — including but not limited to technical
requirements, budget figures, evaluation scores, and internal processes — is confidential.

2.2 Vendors must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) prior to receiving any detailed
technical specifications. The NDA period is **[X] years** from the date of signing.

2.3 Vendors may not disclose that they are participating in this procurement process without
prior written consent from [COMPANY NAME].

---

## 3. Intellectual Property

3.1 Any deliverable, software, design, report, or work product produced under a contract
arising from this RFP shall be considered **work-for-hire** and become the sole property of
[COMPANY NAME] upon payment.

3.2 The vendor retains no rights to use, reproduce, or reference deliverables for marketing
or portfolio purposes without explicit written approval from [COMPANY NAME].

3.3 Vendors must warrant that their proposed solution does not infringe any third-party
intellectual property rights. Any IP infringement liability rests entirely with the vendor.

---

## 4. Liability & Indemnification

4.1 The vendor's total liability under any resulting contract shall not exceed the total
contract value, except in cases of gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or breach of
confidentiality obligations.

4.2 The vendor shall indemnify and hold harmless [COMPANY NAME], its officers, directors,
and employees from any third-party claims arising from the vendor's performance or
non-performance under the contract.

4.3 Neither party shall be liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.

---

## 5. Insurance Requirements

Vendors must maintain the following insurance coverage throughout the contract period and
provide certificates of insurance upon request:

| Coverage Type                  | Minimum Amount          |
|-------------------------------|------------------------|
| General Commercial Liability  | [AMOUNT] per occurrence |
| Professional Indemnity (E&O)  | [AMOUNT] per claim      |
| Cyber Liability (if applicable)| [AMOUNT] per incident  |
| Workers' Compensation         | As required by law      |

---

## 6. Conflict of Interest

6.1 Vendors must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest at the time of bid
submission and throughout the contract period.

6.2 A formal Conflict of Interest Declaration form must be included in every bid submission.
Bids submitted without this declaration will be disqualified automatically.

---

## 7. Anti-Bribery & Compliance

7.1 Vendors must comply with all applicable anti-bribery, anti-corruption, and sanctions laws,
including [RELEVANT LOCAL LAWS, e.g. FCPA, UK Bribery Act, Saudi Anti-Corruption Law].

7.2 Any vendor found to have offered, given, or received any improper inducement will be
immediately disqualified and reported to relevant authorities.

7.3 Vendors must confirm they are not listed on OFAC, UN, or EU sanctions lists. A sanctions
compliance certificate may be requested.

---

## 8. Termination Rights

8.1 [COMPANY NAME] reserves the right to terminate any resulting contract for convenience
with **[X] days** written notice, paying only for work completed and accepted to date.

8.2 [COMPANY NAME] may terminate immediately for cause, including but not limited to:
material breach, insolvency, fraud, or failure to remedy a notified deficiency within
**[X] business days**.

---

## 9. Dispute Resolution

9.1 Parties agree to first attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation for
**30 calendar days**.

9.2 If unresolved, disputes shall be referred to mediation under the rules of [MEDIATION BODY].

9.3 If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration seated in
[CITY, COUNTRY] under [ARBITRATION RULES, e.g. ICC, DIAC, LCIA].

---

## 10. Data Protection

10.1 Vendors processing personal data on behalf of [COMPANY NAME] must comply with
[APPLICABLE DATA PROTECTION LAW, e.g. GDPR, Saudi PDPL, UAE DIFC DP Law].

10.2 A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) must be executed before any personal data is transferred.

10.3 Vendors must notify [COMPANY NAME] of any data breach within **72 hours** of discovery.
