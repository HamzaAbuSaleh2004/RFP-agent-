# Financial Guidelines — Benchmarks, Health Checks & Document Requirements

> These guidelines define how LiverX evaluates the financial dimension of every vendor
> bid. The FMP financial health check runs automatically for all bids. Thresholds below
> are hard-coded into the evaluation agent — no manual override without CFO sign-off.

---

## 1. Market-Rate Benchmarking

1.1 All vendor proposals are benchmarked against **market median rates** for the
    relevant service category. LiverX uses FMP industry data and internal rate history.

1.2 Flagging thresholds:

| Deviation from Market Median | Action |
|---|---|
| Proposal is **>30% above** market median | Flagged for Finance review; vendor must justify premium |
| Proposal is **>30% below** market median | Flagged for risk review; vendor must demonstrate viability |
| Within ±30% of median | Passes benchmark check automatically |

1.3 "Market median" is calculated per category using the most recent 12-month data.
    Categories include: IT Consulting, Software Development, Data Analytics, Cybersecurity,
    Legal Advisory, Management Consulting, and Engineering Services.

---

## 2. Cost-Control Mechanisms

2.1 All engagements must include the following cost-control provisions in the contract:

**Change Order Process:**
   - All scope changes must be documented in a formal **Change Order Request (COR)**
   - No work on changed scope may begin until the COR is signed by both parties
   - Unapproved work will not be reimbursed, regardless of completion

**Budget Cap:**
   - Every contract must specify a **hard budget ceiling** (not-to-exceed amount)
   - Vendor must flag when **75% of the budget ceiling** has been consumed
   - Exceeding the ceiling without a signed Change Order is a material breach

**Monthly Burn Reporting:**
   - Vendors must submit a **monthly budget burn report** by the 5th of each month
   - Report must show: budgeted vs. actual spend, forecast to complete, risk flags
   - Non-submission triggers a payment hold until the report is received

## 3. FMP Financial Health Check Thresholds

The automated FMP evaluation checks the following thresholds using the most recently
available annual financial statements. Results are automatically included in the
evaluation report.

### 3.1 Automatic FAIL Criteria

Any vendor triggering an automatic FAIL is **disqualified from the evaluation** and the
Legal team is automatically notified.

| Metric | Threshold | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash Runway** | < 18 months | **Automatic FAIL** — Legal team alerted immediately |
| **Net Income (latest FY)** | Negative for 2+ consecutive years | **Automatic FAIL** |

### 3.2 Warning Criteria

Vendors triggering a WARNING proceed to evaluation but the flag is included in the
final recommendation report. Procurement and Finance jointly decide whether to proceed.

| Metric | Threshold | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Income (latest FY)** | Negative in most recent year (not consecutive) | **WARNING** — flagged in report |
| **Debt-to-Equity Ratio** | > 3.0 | **WARNING** — financial stability concern |
| **Revenue Trend** | Declining >15% YoY for 2+ years | **WARNING** — business viability concern |
| **Gross Margin** | < 15% for services firms | **WARNING** — pricing sustainability concern |

### 3.3 PASS Criteria

A vendor receives a PASS if none of the above thresholds are triggered.

## 4. Required Financial Documents

All vendors must submit the following with their bid. **Missing documents = evaluation on hold.**

| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Audited Financial Statements** | Last 2 completed fiscal years; signed by registered auditor |
| **Bank Reference Letter** | Issued by a recognised commercial bank; dated within 90 days of bid submission |
| **Proof of Insurance** | Current certificates for all four required coverage types (see Legal Guidelines) |
| **Tax Clearance Certificate** | Confirming no outstanding tax liabilities (Jordan or country of incorporation) |
| **Shareholders / Ownership Declaration** | Beneficial ownership structure; required for contracts >USD 250,000 |

---

## 5. Financial Qualification Thresholds

5.1 Vendors must meet these minimum financial thresholds to qualify for contracts in
    the stated value ranges:

| Contract Value | Minimum Annual Revenue | Minimum Net Worth |
|---|---|---|
| Up to USD 100,000 | 3× contract value | Positive net assets |
| USD 100,000 – USD 500,000 | 4× contract value | USD 200,000 minimum |
| USD 500,000 – USD 2,000,000 | 5× contract value | USD 1,000,000 minimum |
| >USD 2,000,000 | 5× contract value + credit rating BBB or equivalent | USD 5,000,000 minimum |

5.2 For joint ventures or consortium bids, the qualifying entity must meet the
    above thresholds independently — aggregate financials are not accepted.

---

## 6. Payment & Escrow Provisions

6.1 For contracts exceeding **USD 1,000,000**, LiverX may require an **escrow arrangement**
    for the retention amount (typically 10% of contract value), released upon final
    acceptance and expiry of the defects liability period.

6.2 Retention amount and defects liability period are specified per contract; default
    retention is **5%** held for **12 months** post go-live.
