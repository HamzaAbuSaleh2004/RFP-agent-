## 2. Data Governance & Privacy Compliance

2.1 Vendors must comply with all applicable data protection regulations for the jurisdiction
in which services are delivered:

| Region / Country   | Applicable Law                            |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Saudi Arabia       | Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)      |
| UAE                | DIFC Data Protection Law / ADGM DP Regs |
| European Union     | GDPR (Regulation 2016/679)               |
| United Kingdom     | UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018       |
| [OTHER COUNTRY]    | [APPLICABLE LAW]                         |

2.2 Vendors must describe their data residency approach. Data must remain within
**[APPROVED REGIONS / COUNTRIES]** unless explicit written approval is granted.

2.3 Vendors must have a documented data classification and handling policy and provide
a copy upon request.

2.4 Personal data must be encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent).

## 3. Information Security Requirements

3.1 Vendors must complete and return a Vendor Security Assessment Questionnaire (VSAQ)
with their bid. The VSAQ template is available from the Procurement team on request.

3.2 Minimum security controls required:

| Control Area              | Requirement                                        |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| Access Management         | MFA enforced for all privileged accounts           |
| Vulnerability Management  | Critical patches applied within 72 hours           |
| Penetration Testing       | Annual pen test by accredited third party; report available on request |
| Incident Response         | Documented IRP with RTO ≤ [X] hours for critical systems |
| Backup & Recovery         | Daily backups; RPO ≤ [X] hours, RTO ≤ [X] hours   |

3.3 Vendors must notify [COMPANY NAME] of any security incident affecting [COMPANY NAME]
data within **4 hours** of detection and provide a full incident report within **72 hours**.

## 6. Anti-Money Laundering & Sanctions Screening

6.1 All vendors will be screened against the following lists prior to contract award:
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- UN Consolidated Sanctions List
- EU Consolidated Sanctions List
- [LOCAL SANCTIONS LIST, if applicable]

6.2 Vendors must provide a completed Sanctions Self-Declaration Form with their bid.

6.3 Any vendor flagged during screening will be immediately disqualified pending a
compliance review. The procurement team will notify the Legal team within 1 hour of any flag.

## 7. Health, Safety & Environment (HSE) (if applicable)

7.1 For engagements involving physical works, on-site presence, or hazardous materials:
- Vendors must hold a valid HSE Management System certification.
- A site-specific HSE plan must be submitted before work commences.
- LTIFR (Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate) must be below **[X]** per million hours worked.

7.2 All vendor personnel on [COMPANY NAME] premises must complete a site safety induction
before starting work.

## 8. Regulatory Change

8.1 Vendors must monitor applicable regulations throughout the contract period and notify
[COMPANY NAME] promptly of any regulatory change that may affect service delivery,
data handling, or contractual obligations.

8.2 Cost implications of mandatory regulatory changes will be assessed via the Change Order
process described in the Economic Template.
